About Me

Hello! I'm Walter Silima

I am an Astronomy Support Specialist at the Inter-University Institute for Data Intensive Astronomy (IDIA), based in the Department of Astronomy at the University of Cape Town (UCT). My role is to supports researchers in harnessing high-performance computing and advanced data science tools for large-scale radio astronomy projects.

I am also a coordinator for the Data Science for Astronomy, an MSc course which part of the National Astrophysics and Space Science Programme (NASSP), and offered as an elective in the MSc Data Science programme, at UCT. This course bridges astronomy and data science, helping train the next generation of researchers to tackle the challenges of data-intensive science.

My expertise spans machine learning, scientific computing, and containerisation technologies, with a strong focus on enabling efficient and reproducible research in astronomy. I graduated Magna Cum Laude with an MSc in Physical Science from the University of the Western Cape (UWC). I have a broad background in software development, data analysis, radio astronomy data reduction, physics, and chemistry and I am particular interested in applying statistical and machine learning methods to automate key astronomical tasks including source classification and photometric redshift estimation.

My current research explores novel approaches to the computational challenges of wide-field radio interferometric imaging. In particular, I am investigating how physics-informed machine learning methods, such as image transformers, can replace costly regridding operations, improving both image fidelity and computational efficiency. This work has the potential to make wide-field imaging tractable for next-generation radio telescopes such as the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) and the next-generation Very Large Array (ngVLA).
